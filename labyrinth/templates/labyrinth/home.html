The Labyrinth


Every rock has a name, and every tree looks the same.

The cobbled path of 6 birch trees that decide to go bald every winter,

a few vines of english ivy (an invasive species, but these ones doesn't seem to be doing so well at their job),

and about a billion Western hemlock trees is no newer to you than life itself.


Your stepmother has sent you berry hunting... again.

You really wouldn't mind being outside if the outside was as beautiful as it was a year ago.

Mother Nature's cradles has long since been replaced by a drought, and with her absence the flora has suffered.

Maybe that Theta Grunberg was right about climate change and what not...
