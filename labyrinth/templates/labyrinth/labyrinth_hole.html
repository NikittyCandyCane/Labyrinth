The Labrynth


The feeling that pulls you back into your head is soft and serene. Your eyes close and the feeling in your body begins to numb. The sounds you heard around you fade as well.

I like this much more than gaining my senses.

You yawn peacefully and stretch your arms as a white noise fills your ears.

You do this even when you've just woken up? You sure are dedicated to your craft.

"You're still here!". You start out loud.

Good morning to you too.

"Don't expect me to be happy hearing a voice in my head." You say, and begin to worry that you might be schizophrenic just like your stepmother.

Don't worry. I don't think you are. I know you are.

Your nose wrinkles as she lets out a grumpy sound and gets up from where you laid. Big, ripe black mulberries glint in your peripheral vision, asking to be picked. You walk over and pluck them from their branches, when the floor falls out from beneath you! You're falling, falling, falling!!!
